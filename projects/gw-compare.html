GW Compare: Platform Modernization

The Context

The insurance industry is highly competitive, with carriers constantly balancing growth and risk. To stay profitable, businesses race to write more policies while carefully managing losses and exposure.

Understanding performance in this environment requires more than looking at numbers in isolation. Carriers need to compare outcomes across time—year over year—and against peers to make informed strategic decisions about pricing, underwriting, and portfolio management.

Guidewire's Compare tool was built to support this need, enabling insurers to analyze performance trends and benchmark results in order to better understand their overall financial position.

Timeline

Oct 2025 - Nov 2025

Domain

Enterprise UX, Analytics, B2B

Role

UX Designer, Heuristic Evaluation, Prototyping

Team

1 Designers, 4 Developers, 1 Product Manager

Problem Statement

The legacy UI stack constrained consistency, slowed feature development, and made it difficult to evolve interaction patterns alongside Guidewire's modern products. Each enhancement required custom solutions, increasing maintenance cost and fragmenting the user experience across the platform.

PERSONA

Sarah, Business Analyst

How might we...

Transform Guidewire Compare using Jutro such that the most frustrating part of a Sarah's day into a moment of clarity and confidence?

Constraint

Focused on high-impact improvements within constrained scope, prioritizing changes that delivered outsized value without disrupting existing workflows or release timelines.

Compressed delivery timelines, with approximately three weeks of design followed by 2–3 months of development, requiring rapid alignment, decisive trade-offs, and lean iteration.

No direct access to customers or end users, relying on a single subject-matter expert—who also served as the Product Manager—which required strong judgment, careful assumption testing, and a disciplined approach to decision-making under uncertainty.

Approach

Our approach balanced rigor and speed—grounded in heuristics, informed by platform history, and executed through close design–engineering collaboration.

Heuristic Evaluation → Established a baseline of usability debt and surfaced quick-win opportunities.

What we examined
  • Empty / low-value landing states
  • Hidden actions increasing cognitive load
  • Fragmented content blocking insight synthesis
  • Visual inconsistency with Guidewire branding

Platform & Historical Insights → Grounded decisions in institutional knowledge despite no direct user access.

What we leveraged
  • Prior designer research and audits
  • Existing usability feedback and recommendations
  • Known pain points across the Live ecosystem

Comparative & Pattern Analysis → Reduced fragmentation and enabled scalable, system-level consistency.

What we aligned
  • Reusable interaction patterns across products
  • Jutro design system conventions
  • Enterprise analytics interaction standards

Parallel Design–Development Iteration → Faster delivery, fewer handoff gaps, and production-ready outcomes.

How we worked
  • Designed alongside engineering
  • Validated feasibility early
  • Iterated quickly under tight timelines

Project Reflections

Modernization Requires Balancing Innovation with Familiarity

While introducing modern patterns improved usability, maintaining familiar workflows was crucial for user acceptance. The challenge was finding the right balance between innovation and familiarity.

Design Systems Accelerate Consistency

Investing time upfront in building a comprehensive design system paid dividends throughout the project, enabling faster iteration and ensuring visual consistency across all platform updates.

Stakeholder Alignment is Critical

Regular communication and alignment with product managers, developers, and business stakeholders throughout the design process prevented scope creep and ensured successful implementation.

Certain elements of the design can have a significantly greater impact than expected

Elements like blank landing screens aren't just confusing—it starts making Sarah lose confidence in her ability to complete the task on time.

Comparative analysis may not be beneficial when the use cases are vastly different

  • The analysis conducted on the current GW products was beneficial as it allowed us to uphold operational efficiency and consistency standards across our entire product range.
  • The analysis of comparison tools with significantly varied use cases proved to be ineffective in meeting the requirements of our target audience.
