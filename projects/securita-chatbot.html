SecuRita ChatBot

The Context

Securitas Technology delivers advanced security solutions for commercial and residential spaces, including access control, remote monitoring, and intrusion systems. Their flagship app, HQ, provides users with real-time monitoring, instant alert responses, and seamless management of security systems, ensuring effective and proactive security oversight.

During the Covid-19 pandemic, we experienced a drastic 600% increase in customer service calls. This surge led to longer wait times, more abandoned calls, and added stress on our support team. To tackle this challenge, we introduced a conversational user interface bot. Designed with our customers in mind, this bot efficiently handles inquiries, captures user needs, and helps us maintain strong customer relationships, even during peak times.

Key Features

Transparent, Human-Centered Conversations

The chatbot uses a warm, conversational tone while remaining explicit that users are interacting with an automated system—balancing approachability with clarity so expectations are never ambiguous.

Intelligent Dialogues

Rather than relying on a single confirmation, the chatbot reinforces accuracy over time by validating critical details—such as date, time, and location—at multiple points in the conversation.

Expedited Issue Resolution

Routine support tasks are resolved directly within the chatbot, reducing reliance on call centers and speeding up access to information.

Project Reflections

In high-risk environments like security, trust is built through transparency—not personality.

Early explorations leaned toward making the chatbot more conversational and friendly. User feedback quickly showed that in security-critical workflows, clarity and predictability mattered more than charm. Users wanted to know what the system was doing, why it was asking a question, and what would happen next.

Redundancy isn't inefficiency when the cost of error is high.

Confirming date, time, and location multiple times initially felt repetitive. However, testing revealed that this repetition increased confidence, especially when actions affected real-world systems. Users preferred deliberate confirmation over speed when consequences were non-reversible.

The best conversational experiences quietly guide users without making them think about the interface.

While chat interfaces feel informal, successful interactions depended heavily on structured inputs—pickers, buttons, and summaries—rather than free-form text. This reduced ambiguity and helped users move faster without second-guessing their inputs.

Ethical, transparent automation builds more trust than pretending to be human.

Making it clear that users were interacting with an automated system—rather than attempting to simulate a human agent—helped set expectations and reduced confusion. Explicit system messages built confidence instead of diminishing the experience.
