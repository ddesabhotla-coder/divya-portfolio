Insightful UX

The Context

Every day, underwriters and claims adjusters arrive to overflowing queues—new policies to evaluate, claims to review, deadlines that can’t slip. Each decision they make carries real consequences: financial risk for the business, compliance obligations, and outcomes that directly affect customers. To move work forward, they must sift through large volumes of complex information—risk reports, policy history, third-party data, photos, and notes—often scattered across multiple systems. Important details are buried, context is fragmented, and the pressure to act quickly leaves little room for second-guessing. In this environment, the challenge isn’t a lack of data—it’s making the right information visible at the right moment, so experts can focus on judgment instead of searching.

Timeline

2023-2024

Domain

Analytics UX, Fintech, B2B

Role

UX Designer, User Testing, Prototyping

Team

2 Designers, 1 Lead Dev, 1 Product Manager

Problem Statement

How might we reduce cognitive load for underwriters and claims adjusters by simplifying complex data into clear, actionable insights—without disrupting their existing workflows?

✩ Impact

Partnered with Product and Engineering to embed contextual analytics across four enterprise products, transforming standalone reports into in-workflow insights and driving a 25% increase in adoption.

Constraint

  • Legacy architecture limited integrations to iframe-based embedding, requiring solutions that could operate independently while feeling native within host products.
  • Stateless system requirements driven by PII and regulatory constraints, preventing persistence of user or customer data across sessions.
  • Carrier-specific underwriting guidelines that frequently change, necessitating a highly flexible and configurable system rather than fixed logic or hard-coded rules.
  • No access to end users given the b2b nature of the company

✩ Case Study Focus

This initiative embedded insights from four analytics systems into decision-critical points across five enterprise workflows. To keep the narrative focused, this case study highlights the property underwriting use case, while the custom-built configuration platform that powered these experiences will be detailed in a separate case study.

Approach

  1. Understand the use case → Identify decision moments and where insights can reduce effort without disrupting workflows.
  2. Determine critical information → Define hierarchy: crucial signals vs supporting data. Clarify what must be 'glanceable' vs 'drill-down'.
  3. Categorize & Wireframe → Group insights into meaningful sections and choose visuals that best convey risk, confidence, and impact.
  4. User (SME) testing → Validate that information is interpreted as intended and supports real decisions under time pressure.
  5. UX patterns, accessibility & Dev audit → Align reusable patterns across products; audit for accessibility; identify scale-downs due to tech limits.
  6. Hi-fid Design iteration & testing loop → Refine designs based on user feedback and feasibility.
  7. Dev handoff & UX debt loop → 1. Partner through build, document patterns, and track and manage UX debt that arise due to time and dependency constraints

Outcome

"Right information surfaced at the right time"

Project Reflections

In high-stakes systems, clarity builds confidence, but transparency builds trust.

While the Underwriters are looking for instant insights, we still need to provide them a way to verify the details by providing a way to drill down for indicators that ket to that key insight.

Reducing Cognitive Load Means Removing Work

The most impactful outcomes wasn't from adding analytics - it was removing the need to search, compare, and mentally reconcile multiple data sources. By embedding insights directly at decision points, we reduced scanning behavior and helped users stay in flow.

Consistency Scales Faster Than Customization

Once we did the ground work for one of the embedded use cases, we were able to build faster by reusing patterns across solutions

Design Ownership Doesn't End at Handoff

Several design compromises surfaced during development due to performance and integration constraints. Rather than treating these as failures, we tracked them as UX debt and documented future improvements, ensuring design intent wasn't lost over time.
